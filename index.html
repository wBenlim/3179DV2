Melbourne & NSW Tourism and Economic Growth across 2014 to 2024

The visualisation dashboard below shows the overview of tourism performance and its impact on regional economic development on Victoria and New South Wales, Australia from 2014 - 2024. The purpose of this dashboard is to allow users to visualize tourism and economic trends such as the overall number of visitors, the number of jobs filled due to tourism and the gross regional product which is the GDP of a region; over the years in the regions of Melbourne, Victoria. Users can also compare the tourism and economic developement between 2 states of Australia, Victoria and New South Wales

Tourism & Economic developement Map of Melbourne regions

Melbourne regional tourism and economic growth from 2014 - 2024
The map above shows from 2014 to 2024 3 tourism indictors: Gross value added, Gross regional product and number of tourist. This indictors are plotted on a map of melbourne and will compare the regions of melbourne. The indictor can be selected using the dropdown menu of tourism_indicator. The first slider for the year can be used to show the value of the indictor for that year. As this is a symbol map, the larger the bubble on the map, the larger the value of the tourism indictor is at that place on the map. The larger the value is, the darker the orange bubble is on the map. There is also a zoom function with the slider at the end of the map to zoom in and out of the map.
Victoria vs New south wales economic development
The line chart on the right shows the comparison of the economic metric which is selected at the end of the line chart with a dropdown menu; of Victoria and new south wales. The brush which is the chart below the line chart can be used to select the year or the period of the time in years to filter the line chart and compare victoria and new south wales.
